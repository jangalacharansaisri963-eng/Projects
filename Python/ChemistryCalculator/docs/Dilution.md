# Dilution

Dilution is the process of making a solution less concentrated by adding more solvent.

In basic chemistry, the solvent is usually water.

During dilution:

- The amount of solute remains the same.
- The total volume increases.
- The concentration decreases.


The reason concentration decreases is because the same amount of dissolved substance is spread over a larger volume.

Molarity is:

\[
M=\frac{n}{V}
\]

Since the number of moles does not change during dilution, increasing volume decreases molarity.


The dilution formula is:

\[
M_1V_1=M_2V_2
\]

where:

M₁ = original concentration

V₁ = original volume

M₂ = final concentration

V₂ = final volume


Example:

A 5 M solution has a volume of 100 mL. It needs to be diluted to 1 M.

Using:

\[
M_1V_1=M_2V_2
\]

\[
5 \times 100=1 \times V_2
\]

\[
V_2=500mL
\]


The final solution must have a volume of 500 mL.

The amount of water needed is:

\[
Water=V_2-V_1
\]

\[
Water=500-100
\]

\[
Water=400mL
\]


In Chemistry Calculator, dilution is performed using:

dilution(M1, V1, M2)


The command returns:

- Final solution volume
- Amount of water required


Example:

dilution(5,100,1)


Result:

Final volume: 500 mL

Water added: 400 mL


Dilution is commonly used in chemistry laboratories when preparing solutions with lower concentrations from stronger solutions.
