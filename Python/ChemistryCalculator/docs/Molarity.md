# Molarity

Molarity is a measurement of the concentration of a solution.

It tells us how many moles of solute are present in a certain volume of solution.

The formula for molarity is:

\[
M=\frac{n}{V}
\]

where:

M = molarity (mol/L)

n = number of moles of solute

V = volume of solution in liters


For example, if 2 moles of sodium chloride are dissolved to make 4 liters of solution:

\[
M=\frac{2}{4}
\]

\[
M=0.5M
\]

This means every liter of solution contains 0.5 moles of sodium chloride.


Molarity connects the amount of substance with the volume of the solution. It is commonly used in:

- Chemical reactions in solutions
- Acid and base calculations
- Laboratory preparation
- Dilution calculations


The formula can also be rearranged.

To find moles:

\[
n=MV
\]

To find volume:

\[
V=\frac{n}{M}
\]


In Chemistry Calculator, molarity calculations are performed using:

molarity()

This calculates concentration from moles and volume.

moles_from_molarity()

This calculates the number of moles from concentration and volume.

volume_from_molarity()

This calculates the required volume from moles and concentration.


The standard unit of molarity is:

\[
mol/L
\]

Although other units may be used in chemistry, mol/L is the most common unit for solution concentration.
